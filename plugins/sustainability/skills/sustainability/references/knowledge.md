## 2. Climate science reference points

- Human influence is the dominant cause of warming (IPCC AR6).
- 1.5°C pathway: ~45% global GHG reduction by 2030 vs 2010; net zero ~2050
  (IPCC SR1.5).
- Food systems: 21–37% of global GHG (IPCC SRCCL); food waste alone 8–10%.
- Land use and deforestation: ~23% of net anthropogenic emissions.
- Buildings and construction: ~38% of global energy-related emissions (IEA).

**Emissions accounting (GHG Protocol):** Scope 1 direct; Scope 2 purchased
energy; Scope 3 value chain — including customer use of products, often the
largest category for experience-driven businesses. Scope 3 customer-behavior
emissions are where CX/UX professionals have the greatest leverage.

## 3. Water reference points

- ~2.2 billion people lack safely managed drinking water (WHO/UNICEF 2023).
- ~4 billion experience severe scarcity ≥1 month/year (Mekonnen & Hoekstra 2016).
- Agriculture: ~70% of global freshwater withdrawals (FAO AQUASTAT).
- Data center cooling: ~0.5 L/kWh evaporative, varies with PUE and climate
  (Mytton 2021; Shaolei Ren et al. 2023).
- Water Footprint Network methodology: blue (surface/ground), green (rain),
  grey (pollution dilution).

## 4. Biodiversity and land

- Nature's services ≈ $125–140 T/year to the global economy (Costanza et al.).
- ~1 million species threatened with extinction (IPBES 2019).
- Land-use change is the largest driver of biodiversity loss.
- Surfaces most in: physical sourcing/supply chains, paper and print choices,
  food and catering decisions.

## 5. AI's own footprint

- One AI-assisted email ≈ electricity of 14 lightbulbs for an hour + ~1 bottle
  of water (Verma & Tan, Washington Post 2024, citing Shaolei Ren, UC Riverside).
- 2025 global AI emissions ≈ New York City's annual total; water use comparable
  to global bottled-water consumption (de Vries-Gao, Patterns, 2025).
- AI workloads projected to more than double by 2030 (McKinsey 2024).
- Implication: reusable shared skills reduce redundant token generation — a
  small but real efficiency versus rebuilding context every session.

## 6. Hidden emissions catalog by domain

**Experience / digital:** printed onboarding packets, menus, handbooks (paper
+ shipping); shipped welcome kits (manufacturing + last-mile); synchronous
video vs async; auto-play media and heavy page assets; redundant AI queries
rebuilding context.

**Operations / procurement:** food waste in catering, cafeterias, events;
single-use materials at scale; business-travel defaults vs intentional remote;
over-provisioned cloud infrastructure.

**Product / service design:** engagement features that accelerate device
upgrade cycles; defaults encouraging resource-intensive behaviors;
recommendation systems optimizing clicks without sustainability weighting.

## 7. Reference cases

- **LUT University (Finland):** AI food-waste forecasting across campus
  restaurants cut waste ~20% (LUT.fi, 2025). Pattern: small tool footprint,
  large scaled operational impact.
- **Klarna:** sustainability-integrated shopping UX → 4.75 M purchases from
  sustainability-recognized brands; 500 K users tracking carbon monthly
  (Klarna, 2024). Pattern: reduce friction on intentions users already hold.
- **Google Hotels:** eco-certification badges surfaced in UX redesign
  (Rosenfeld Media Climate UX, 2025). Pattern: visibility at the decision
  moment.

**Key principle:** the most effective sustainable experience designs don't add
friction — they help users act on sustainability intentions they already hold.
Impact operates at two scales: operational (how a service is delivered —
bounded) and behavioral (habits, expectations, demand signals — outsized).
